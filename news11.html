Beautiful-Sounding Words Boost Word Retention

A study conducted by researchers has found that words with more pleasant sounds are easier to remember. The research explored how sound alone, independent of meaning, influences perception and memory. Around 100 English-speaking participants were presented with invented words such as “clisious,” “smanious,” and “drikious.” These made-up words were carefully designed to sound either “beautiful,” “neutral,” or “not beautiful.”

Participants first learned and memorized the words, then attempted to recall them, and finally rated how aesthetically pleasing they found each word. The results showed a clear pattern: the words that participants remembered most accurately were also those they rated as the most beautiful. According to the study leader, the findings highlight a strong relationship between memorability and euphony—the quality of being pleasing to the ear.

However, the researchers noted that it remains unclear whether people remember words better because they sound beautiful, or whether they perceive them as beautiful because they are easier to remember. The findings suggest that sound aesthetics may influence foreign language learning, marketing, and even long-term language change across generations in modern societies over time.
