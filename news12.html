Daily Life Within Shared Communal Living Spaces

As living costs rise and loneliness becomes a growing global issue, intentional communities and co-living arrangements are gaining popularity. With high housing and rental prices, especially in major cities, more people are exploring communal living as an alternative to traditional housing. Growing recognition of loneliness as a public health concern has further highlighted the appeal of shared living spaces.

One resident, a 30-year-old food writer based in London, moved into a warehouse-style communal home after a personal life change in 2020. She now lives with six others in their late twenties and early thirties. Unlike typical shared housing, their home operates with structured systems: each member contributes a weekly amount to cover shared expenses, and one person cooks dinner each night. They use group chats to coordinate meals and guests, and hold regular house meetings to address chores and concerns before tensions build.

She says the greatest benefit is constant companionship and a sense of family, particularly in a city where affordable housing is scarce. However, communal living also presents challenges, including limited privacy, noise, and sharing facilities such as bathrooms and a single washing machine.
