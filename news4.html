Three Years Left to Limit Global Warming

More than 60 leading scientists warn that the world could exceed the 1.5°C global warming limit within the next three years if carbon dioxide emissions continue at today’s pace.

In 2020, scientists estimated that the world could emit about 500 billion tonnes more carbon dioxide and still have a 50% chance of staying below 1.5°C. By early 2025, that “carbon budget” had shrunk to just 130 billion tonnes because emissions had accelerated. With the world releasing around 40 billion tonnes each year, this budget could run out in about three years.

Last year was the first time global temperatures stayed above 1.5°C for an entire year, mainly due to human-driven warming. The current rate of temperature rise, about 0.27°C per decade, is faster than anything seen in Earth’s history.

About 90% of the extra heat is absorbed by the oceans, harming marine life and speeding up sea-level rise, which has doubled since the 1990s.

Scientists say the world cannot rely on future carbon removal technologies to fix the problem. Rapid and deep emission cuts are needed now, as every fraction of a degree avoided will reduce future harm.
