Three Years Left to Limit Global Warming

More than 60 leading scientists warn the world could breach the 1.5°C warming limit within three years if carbon dioxide emissions remain at current levels.

In 2020, the remaining “carbon budget” for a 50% chance of staying below 1.5°C was estimated at 500 billion tonnes of CO₂. By early 2025, this had fallen to 130 billion tonnes due to sustained emissions and refined calculations. At current levels of about 40 billion tonnes a year, the budget could be exhausted in roughly three years, though the threshold would likely be crossed later.

Last year marked the first time global temperatures stayed above 1.5°C for 12 consecutive months, largely due to human-driven warming. The current rate, 0.27°C per decade, is far faster than anything in geological history.

Around 90% of excess heat is absorbed by oceans, disrupting ecosystems and accelerating sea-level rise, which has doubled since the 1990s. Scientists caution against relying on future CO₂ removal technologies to reverse warming, warning they may not fully offset the damage.

Experts stress rapid, stringent emissions cuts are essential, noting that every fraction of avoided warming reduces harm, from extreme weather to rising seas.
