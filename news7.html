Few Years Left to Limit Global Warming

Leading climate scientists’ states that the Earth could breach the critical 1.5C warming threshold within just a few years if carbon emissions continue at current levels. The 1.5C limit was established to avoid the most dangerous impacts of global warming, yet emissions from coal, oil and gas remain near record highs, pushing that goal increasingly out of reach. Remaining global carbon budget has shrunk dramatically as greenhouse gas emissions persist. At current emission rates, it could be exhausted within a short timeframe, effectively causing further warming.

Scientists warn that although temporary temperature spikes do not constitute a formal breach, the long-term trend is clear: human-driven warming is accelerating. The planet is now warming at a pace far faster than historical natural changes. Heat is accumulating rapidly in the Earth’s system, with oceans absorbing the majority of excess energy. This intensifies marine disruption, accelerates glacier melt and drives sea-level rise, increasing flood risks for millions living in coastal areas.

Experts stress that only rapid and substantial emissions cuts can slow the trajectory, every fraction of a degree matters. Immediate action to reduce greenhouse gases is essential to limit worsening extremes, protect vulnerable communities and prevent irreversible climate damage.
