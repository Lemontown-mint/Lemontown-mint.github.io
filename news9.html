Global Growth Forecast Slows to 2.3%

The global economy is projected to slow to 2.3% growth in 2025, slipping below the 2.5% level often associated with recessionary conditions.

The downturn is being driven by escalating trade tensions, financial volatility, and rising policy uncertainty. These factors have disrupted supply chains, delayed investment decisions, and weakened hiring, creating what analysts describe as a recessionary trajectory. This marks a sharp deceleration from already sluggish growth seen before the pandemic.

Vulnerabilities are particularly evident in developing regions, where high debt levels, external pressures, and weakening domestic growth pose serious risks. Some economists warn these conditions could form a “perfect storm” of challenges that threaten stability.

At the same time, new opportunities are emerging. Regional economic integration and stronger trade between developing markets are increasingly viewed as potential sources of resilience. Analysts argue that such collaboration, alongside more consistent international coordination, is critical to restoring confidence and supporting long-term development.

The report emphasizes that timely, coordinated policy action will be essential to prevent deeper declines in global growth and to ensure weaker economies are not left behind.
